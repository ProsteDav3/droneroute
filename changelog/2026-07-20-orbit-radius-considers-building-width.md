### Fixed

- A building orbit's radius and its locked-POI clearance guide only ever checked that the building's roofline fit inside the camera's vertical field of view — never that the building's own width (as seen from a given bearing) fit inside the horizontal field of view. For an elongated building, this could leave the roofline comfortably framed while still cropping the building's left/right edges out of the shot, even at a radius that already looked generous. Even a simple square building needs more standoff at a corner-facing bearing than a straight-on one, since the apparent width there is the full diagonal, not one side. Both the recommended radius for a building-derived orbit and the drag clamp/guide circle around a locked POI now also grow to fit the building's width, whichever bearing needs the most standoff.
