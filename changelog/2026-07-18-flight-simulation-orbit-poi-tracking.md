### Fixed

- The previous POI-tracking fix only applied to `headingMode: "towardPOI"` legs, but templates like Orbit don't actually use that mode — they precompute each individual waypoint's heading/pitch correctly toward the subject, then save it as `headingMode: "fixed"` with the numbers baked in (changing that would change what gets written to the real WPML flight file, out of scope for a simulation-only fix). That meant the simulation still had no way to know two "fixed" waypoints were aiming at the same nearby subject, and kept linearly interpolating their static angles between them — drifting off a building mid-orbit exactly like before, even after the previous fix. The simulation now recognizes when a real POI in the mission would explain both endpoints' own precomputed angles, and dynamically tracks it for that leg too, without needing any change to how templates generate or export waypoints.
