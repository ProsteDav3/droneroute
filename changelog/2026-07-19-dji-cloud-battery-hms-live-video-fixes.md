### Fixed

- The aircraft's battery percentage shown in the DJI Cloud panel (both in the device list and the telemetry HUD) could read several points lower than the drone's actual battery — it was reading the platform's separate aggregate `battery.capacity_percent` estimate rather than the physical battery's own reported value, and real OSD data shows these can diverge noticeably even on a single-battery aircraft. Now shows the physical battery reading (averaged across batteries for a dual-battery aircraft), matching what DJI Pilot 2 itself displays.
- HMS warnings from a previous session (e.g. a battery-critical code from two days ago) still showed next to genuinely current ones as long as the device was online, since the platform's HMS history has no "resolved" concept and being currently online doesn't mean a warning is from _this_ connection. Warnings older than the device's current login time are no longer shown.
- A remote controller/gateway device with no platform-reported name showed its bare serial number in the device list. It now shows "DJI Pilot 2" — in practice this case is almost always a tablet/phone running the app itself as the cloud gateway rather than a dedicated RC unit, which does report its own model name.
- Starting a live video feed still failed after the previous `videoId` fix. The platform reports the same `video_index` (e.g. `"normal-0"`) for every lens on a payload — an M4T's wide and normal video entries are indistinguishable by index, only by `video_type` — but the stream identifier was still being built from that unreliable index. It's now built from the video's own type instead, matching the format the platform's `VideoId` parser actually expects.
- The 3D flythrough camera could still render as a smeared near-ground view specifically on a mission at a location the map had never rendered in 3D before (a fresh address, never previously orbited) — its terrain data had never been fetched at all, and the previous retry budget (3s) didn't always cover a cold fetch. The retry window for the flythrough's ground-elevation lookup is longer now.
