# Risk assessment and permits

A lightweight, simplified aid for thinking through operational risk and tracking authorization paperwork per mission — not a substitute for an official SORA submission or legal advice.

## SORA-lite risk assessment

- Fill in a short questionnaire for a saved mission:
  - **Ground risk** — how populated is the area under the flight path (low / medium / high).
  - **Air risk** — VLOS/BVLOS, maximum altitude, and proximity to controlled airspace (low / medium / high).
  - **Mitigations** — checkboxes for common risk-reducing measures: ground observer, safety net, parachute system, geofencing, reduced-area operations.
- Save the assessment; it's shown again next time you open the mission, along with when it was last assessed.
- This is explicitly a simplified planning aid, not an authoritative SORA (Specific Operations Risk Assessment) submission.

## Permit and authorization tracking

- Record authorization/coordination documents against a mission: a description, an optional reference number or URL, an optional expiry date, and who issued it.
- Permits are listed ordered by expiry date, so the ones expiring soonest show first.
- When a permit is expired or expiring within 14 days, a warning banner appears at the bottom of the map alongside the other flight warnings.

## Preflight checklist

- Download a one-page PDF preflight checklist summarizing: airspace conflicts found for the route, mission validation warnings (battery, obstacles), a weather go/no-go read, and the mission's SORA-lite risk assessment and permit status.
- If no risk assessment or permits have been recorded yet, the checklist says so plainly rather than leaving the section blank.
- The weather go/no-go uses simple wind/precipitation thresholds as a starting point — treat it as a sanity check, not a certified safety clearance.

## Good to know

- Both the risk assessment and permits require the mission to be saved first.
- The field sets are intentionally modest — this is meant as a quick aid, not a replacement for your organization's full safety case or regulator-facing paperwork.
