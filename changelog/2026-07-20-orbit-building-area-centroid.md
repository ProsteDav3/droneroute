### Fixed

- A building orbit's center could sit visibly off to one side of the building instead of over it, for any footprint whose drawn outline has more corners on one side than another (a detailed or stepped wall versus a single straight one) — the center was a plain average of the footprint's vertices, which skews toward whichever side happens to have more of them, not the shape's real area center. Every clearance and framing calculation the orbit's radius depends on assumes the circle is actually centered on the building, so an off-center circle left the building closer to the flight circle on one side than the recommended radius accounted for, no matter how generous that radius looked from the (wrong) center. The center is now the footprint's true area centroid instead.
