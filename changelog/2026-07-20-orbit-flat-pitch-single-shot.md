### Changed

- A building orbit's gimbal pitch no longer varies per waypoint. An earlier revision adapted pitch to each waypoint's real distance to the building's nearest edge so a non-circular footprint stayed framed all the way around — but with the radius now grown to keep every bearing comfortably within the camera's FOV (see the last few releases), that adaptation just made the gimbal visibly tilt up and down over the course of the flight instead of holding one steady view. Every waypoint of an orbit now shares a single flat pitch, computed once.
- Fixed a related issue in the 3D flythrough: even with a flat baked-in pitch, the simulated camera could still drift because a leg's dynamic point-tracking (used to keep the camera aimed at a subject continuously between waypoints) was based on the interpolated position's distance to the target — which shrinks and grows along the straight line between two waypoints even though both waypoints themselves sit at the correct, identical distance. Orbit legs now interpolate pitch directly between the two waypoints' own values instead, which stays flat when — as is now the normal case — those values already agree.
