### Fixed

- A building orbit's recommended altitude could still land below the building's own roofline even after the radius was floored to the building's height (previous release) — for a wide building, the recommended radius itself pushes the whole-object framing math past the point where its ideal margin is achievable at any altitude, and the underlying formula then converges toward half the building's height instead of a comfortable above-the-roof view, no matter how large the radius grows. New orbit recommendations now favor a fixed comfortable altitude above the roofline in that situation, accepting a narrower (but still whole-building) framing margin instead of the mathematically "optimal" but visually confusing below-the-roof compromise. Live edits to an existing orbit's radius/altitude in the panel are unaffected — they still resolve exactly as before.
